Section 01
Vision & Pillars
What Ethereal Convergence is and what it stands for
Concept Statement

Ethereal Convergence is a third-person action RPG set in a fragmented multiverse born from a cosmic calculation error — the equivalent of √(−1) in a deterministic system. Players control the Iterant, the only being capable of moving between Realities, uncovering why they exist and what it means to correct an error that would erase everything.

The game draws inspiration from Genshin Impact, Wuthering Waves, and Honkai: Star Rail in terms of combat feel and character depth — while carving its own identity through a science-fiction multiverse framework, a gacha-free monetization model, and characters whose designs emerge directly from their narrative purpose.

"Every character in Ethereal Convergence carries something. The gameplay is how they express it."

Design Pillars
Narrative Coherence
Every system, character, and mechanic connects back to the lore. Nothing exists in isolation. The Condensation equipment system reflects the multiverse. Character kits reflect their personalities.
Respect for the Player
No predatory monetization. No power gaps between paying and free players. F2P players have access to a complete game experience. Paying players get content earlier and cosmetics — not advantages.
Character Depth
Each Resonant carries an emotional core that informs their design, combat role, and narrative arc. Characters are not interchangeable — they are irreplaceable.
Target Audience

Players aged 16–30 who enjoy narrative-driven ARPGs with deep character rosters. Fans of Genshin Impact and Wuthering Waves who feel underserved by aggressive gacha monetization. Players who invest emotionally in characters and lore.

Secondary audience: players interested in build optimization through the Condensation system and character kit synergies.

Section 02
World & Lore
The universe, its structure, and the rules that govern it
The Origin

The universe of Ethereal Convergence was born from a calculation error — a result equivalent to √(−1) in a deterministic cosmic system. Rather than collapsing, the system fragmented into parallel dimensions called Realities, each one a different interpretation of the original fault.

At the center of everything lies the Point of Convergence — the exact location where the error was detected. Correcting it would erase every Reality that emerged from it. Everything and everyone in the game exists because of a mistake that no one dares undo.

The Realities
Primary Realities
Matter — stability, structure, physical law
Entropy — decay, degradation, inevitable endings
Distortion — malleable reality, rewritten conditions
Void — the space between things, disconnection
Symphony — balance, equilibrium, resonant harmony
Emergent Realities
Chronos — temporal fragmentation
Annulment — negation of existence
Origin — the primordial signal

Emergent Realities formed after the primary fragmentation. Their nature is more unstable and less understood than the primaries.
The Kyleon System & Umbraciel

The Kyleon System is the external infrastructure that monitors the simulation from outside its boundaries. It does not intervene — it observes. Kyleon is not a villain but a force of scientific indifference: it views each death of the Iterant as another data point in an ongoing experiment.

Umbraciel is the location closest to the original error — where the Iterant returns after each death via Ethereal Re-anchoring. It functions as the game's narrative hub.

Resonants

Resonants are beings with a deep affinity to a single Reality. Their resonance is internal, not geographical — a Resonant can be attuned to Entropy while living their entire life in Matter. Resonance is something that happens to someone, shaped by who they are and what they lived through.

Resonants cannot travel between Realities on their own. Only specific factions and the Iterant possess that ability — making the Iterant uniquely valuable and uniquely dangerous to the established order of every Reality.

Section 03
The Iterant
The protagonist — the being that should not exist
Nature & Role

The Iterant is a single consciousness capable of synchronizing with multiple Realities simultaneously — something impossible for any other being. When they die, their consciousness is returned to Umbraciel via Ethereal Re-anchoring. The Kyleon System treats each death as an iteration of an experiment.

Their sentenced desire: understand why they exist. At the start of the game, their only motivation is survival — they don't know how they came to be, how they exist, or how they keep existing.

"The Iterant is the only character in the game without a color of their own at the start. Their palette fills as they synchronize with Realities."

Anchor & Conduit — Two Manifestations
Anchor — Manifestation α — Male
Apparent age 25–27. Cold, calculated, internally insecure. Carries the question of his existence as weight — builds external order to compensate for internal chaos. Black hair with amber reflections under ethereal light. Structured armor, earth tones. Resonance Mark uses uniform strokes and defined edges — "as if engraved under pressure."
Conduit — Manifestation β — Female
Apparent age 21–23. Expressive, curious, fluid — same existential insecurity underneath. Carries the question as current — converts it into speed rather than building walls. Silver-white hair with cyan highlights in motion. Asymmetric clothing, deep blue tones. Resonance Mark uses variable stroke width, finer at the ends — "as if it flowed and settled."

The player chooses their manifestation at the start of the game. This choice is permanent and affects all subsequent story interactions and character dynamics.

Resonance Mark System

The Iterant's Resonance Mark is located on their spine — a place they cannot see directly. Others see it before they do.

  • Raw state: Empty outline ◌ — the shape of something that doesn't exist yet. Completely desaturated palette.
  • First synchronization — Matter: Activates in combat through instinct. Symbol ⊞. Color arrives for the first time.
  • Evolution: The mark deepens with each new Reality — more detail in the same location, never expanding outward.
  • Symphony's blue (#5AA8D8) is the only color identical in both Anchor and Conduit palettes — the visual thread connecting them.
  • Final state: Undefined — depends on the player's decision at the Point of Convergence.

Weapon Types

All weapons are objects that exist in the world — they can be found, dropped, farmed, or crafted. Characters have a weapon of affinity but no weapon is permanently exclusive to any character.

  • Fractal Sword — A blade with a central fracture that emits ethereal light. The fracture is not damage — it is part of the edge. Affinity: Iterant.
  • Coded Rifle — Each shot rewrites a property of the target. Does not destroy — redefines conditions. Affinity: Claire.
  • Compression Greatsword — Accumulates energy per hit, releases in area impacts. Slow and inevitable. Affinity: Valerius.
  • Architect's Compass — Builds geometric shields that distribute damage by impact geometry. Affinity: Liora.
  • Binary Daggers — Risk-accumulation system: damage received scales the next explosive attack. Affinity: Kael.
  • Precision Crossbow — Same weapon, opposite logic. Caelum targets structural break points to disable; Aria targets damage trajectories to intercept. Affinity: Caelum, Aria.
  • Nexus Lance — Strikes energetic bridges between targets, not the targets themselves. Disconnects rather than destroys. Affinity: Silas.
Section 05
Core Systems
Combat, equipment, progression and stats
Combat Overview

Ethereal Convergence uses a real-time action combat system with a party of up to four characters. Players switch between characters on the fly, chaining abilities and building synergies across different Realities.

Each character has four abilities — Basic Attack, Resonance Skill, Reaction, and Ultimate — and seven passive upgrades called Sequences that deepen each character's kit. Combat feel is designed around character identity: Kael is fast and explosive, Valerius is slow and inevitable, Silas is methodical and structural.

Condensations — Equipment System

Condensations are farmable equipment objects that amplify a character's resonance — solidified ethereal energy that took physical form. They integrate directly into a character's equipment with no visual change to the character model. All Condensations are obtainable through gameplay using Ethereal Flow.

Each set consists of 4 pieces across 4 slots, with bonuses at 2 pieces and at full set (4 pieces). The 4-piece bonuses for all sets are pending final balance pass.

Ethereal Flow — Stamina System

Ethereal Flow is the stamina resource used to farm Condensations and upgrade materials. It regenerates over time and can be optionally replenished through premium items — but base regeneration is designed to cover regular daily play without requiring refills.

The system is designed to feel enabling, not restrictive. Premium refills exist as an option, never as a necessity.

Section 06
Monetization
Non-predatory. Sustainable. Respectful of player time.
Philosophy

Ethereal Convergence is built around a gacha-free model. No random character pulls. No banner system. No artificial urgency. The game should feel complete for every player regardless of spending — and richer for those who choose to support it.

"A well-built team with free characters and farmable Condensations should clear any content in the game. That is not a design failure — it is the design goal."

Revenue Streams
StreamDescriptionF2P Impact
Free Frequency
Battle Pass — Free Tier		 Free seasonal pass available to all players. Completing it to the maximum rewards a 5★ Resonant of the player's choice from the season's available pool. None — fully accessible
Primordial Frequency
Battle Pass — Premium Tier		 Paid seasonal pass with additional rewards, materials, and exclusive cosmetics. Completing it to the maximum rewards a weapon of the player's choice from the season's pool — independent of the character chosen. Characters available in shop after season at fixed price
Cosmetic Shop Character skins, ability visual effects, color variants. Zero gameplay impact. None — purely aesthetic
Ethereal Flow Refills Optional stamina replenishment for players who want to farm more in a single session. Never required for regular play. Minimal — base regeneration covers normal play
Free-to-Play Commitment
  • All story content is free and fully accessible without spending
  • All 5★ Resonants released via Battle Pass become available in the shop after the season — no permanent exclusives
  • No stat-based power gaps between paid and free Condensation sets
  • Free characters (Claire, Kael) are designed to be genuinely competitive — not consolation prizes
  • Ethereal Flow base regeneration covers regular daily play without requiring premium refills

Open Roles
3D Character Artist

Model and rig original characters following established visual design guidelines. Style reference: Genshin Impact / Wuthering Waves anime-adjacent 3D. Character sheets and palette documentation provided for all 8 characters.

Priority role.

Unity Developer (C#)

Build core systems: character controller, combat framework, ability system, Condensation equipment logic, Ethereal Flow stamina system. Full design documentation provided.

Priority role. Unity 2022 LTS experience preferred.

Environment / Level Artist

Design and build environments reflecting the aesthetic of each Reality — from the structured geometry of Matter to the incomplete forms of the Void.

UI / UX Designer

Design game interface consistent with established visual language. Reference documents and color systems provided for all characters and Realities.

Animator

Character animations for combat, movement, and cutscenes. Each character's movement style is defined by their personality — documentation provided per character.

Composer / Sound Designer

Original soundtrack and sound effects. Each Reality has a defined sonic identity — from the structured weight of Matter to the oscillating tension of Symphony.

Revenue Share Model

All collaborators participate in a revenue share agreement. If and when the game generates revenue, earnings are distributed among all contributing team members. Specific percentages are negotiated individually based on role scope and contribution.

This is a passion-driven project. There is no upfront payment. The commitment expected is genuine interest in the project's vision.
